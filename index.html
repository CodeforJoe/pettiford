Oscar Pettiford

The Prototypical Modern Jazz Bassist

Oscar Pettiford was a seminal modern jazz bassist, influencing many prominent bassists in the 1940's and 50's

Timeline of Oscar Pettiford's life

  • 1922- Born in Okmulgee, OK
  • 1941- Encouraged by bassist Milt Hinton to pursue music professionally
  • 1942-Joined the Charlie Barnet Band
  • 1943-Records "The Man I Love" with Coleman Hawkins, co-leads an early Bebop group
    with Dizzy Gillespie
  • 1945-48-Performs with Duke Ellington's band
  • 1949-Performs with Woody Herman Band, begins playing cello tuned in 4ths
  • 1958-Moved to Copenhagen, Denmark to record and perform
  • 1960-Dies from Polio related virus
